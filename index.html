📘 Technical Strategy Specifications

1. Overview & Data Source

This tool compares 17 strategy variants — differing in leverage bounds, rebalancing frequency, and asset composition — against the S&P 500. The core idea: buy more leverage when the market is deeply discounted, de-leverage when near all-time highs. All simulations begin on July 1, 2002 — chosen to capture the post-dot-com recovery, the 2008 financial crisis, the 2020 COVID crash, and the 2022 bear market. Data is refreshed automatically every night via GitHub Actions.

All returns are Total Return (dividends and interest reinvested). The data is sourced from Yahoo Finance using the following proxies:

Leveraged returns are simulated as: Return = (Leverage × SPY) − ((Leverage − 1) × VFISX). This models the real-world borrowing cost of maintaining a leveraged position. Inflation data is fetched monthly from the St. Louis Fed and interpolated daily.

2. Asset Allocation Tier Matrices

Each strategy dynamically shifts its portfolio weights based on the previous day's drawdown (distance from All-Time High). This prevents look-ahead bias — you only act on yesterday's information.

3. Individual Strategy Descriptions

Standard Daily Safeties

Our official "Ladder" model. Scales internal equity leverage from 1.0x (T0) to 4.0x (T4). To keep risk under control, this variant reduces total leverage by 0.8x and shifts that capital into a Commodities/Treasury safety buffer.

Standard Daily Pure

The "Growth Ladder" at full intensity. Maintains 100% equity exposure at all times, ramping leverage from 1x to 4x (using VOO/SSO/SPYU) as drawdowns deepen. Fast recovery, high volatility.

Standard Ratchet Safeties

Uses the 1x-4x Ladder weights with a path-dependent lock-in logic. It refuses to de-leverage until a brand new All-Time High is reached. Includes the 0.8x leverage reduction safety net.

Standard Ratchet Pure

The "Growth Ladder" but with ratchet lock-in logic and 100% equities. Maximum aggression during recoveries as it locks in the 4x leverage tier until parity is restored.

Aggressive Daily Safeties

Uses much tighter drawdown bounds: 3%, 7%, 12%, 20%. Leverage kicks in much earlier. Includes the 20% safety net.

Aggressive Daily Pure

Same tight bounds as Aggressive (3/7/12/20) but with 100% equity exposure. Maximum responsiveness to tiny dips.

Aggressive Ratchet Safeties

Combines Aggressive bounds with the Ratchet lock-in. Includes the 20% safety net.

Aggressive Ratchet Pure

The most hyper-aggressive portfolio. Tightest bounds, Ratchet lock-in, and 100% equity exposure.

Conservative Daily Safeties

Uses very wide drawdown bounds: 10%, 20%, 35%, 50%. This "patient hunter" barely leverages up until a major crash. Includes the 20% safety net.

Conservative Daily Pure

Same wide bounds as Conservative (10/20/35/50) but with 100% equity exposure. No safety net.

Conservative Ratchet Safeties

Combines patient Conservative bounds with Ratchet lock-in. Includes the 20% safety net.

Conservative Ratchet Pure

Uses Conservative bounds, Ratchet lock-in, and 100% equity exposure. Stays quiet during small dips but recovers massively.

Benchmark SPY (1x)

Buy-and-hold the S&P 500 index. No leverage, no rebalancing. The baseline benchmark.

Benchmark SSO (2x)

Static 2x daily leverage. Included to show the compounding drag of constant leverage.

Benchmark SPYU (4x)

Static 4x daily leverage. Included to demonstrate extreme volatility risk.

🏆 Special BEAST

The result of a high-intensity global search. Maximizes raw wealth at **~20% CAGR** (beating the 2x Benchmark's 14.7%) by concentrating leverage in Tier 2 and Tier 4 regimes. This is the "honest" mathematical ceiling for this asset universe.

🎯 Special SCALPEL

Optimized for ultra-smooth growth. Achieves an impressive **0.91 Sharpe Ratio** while maintaining steady 8-9% returns with minimal (-16%) drawdowns. Designed for maximum capital preservation.

🛡️ Special SHIELD

The best of both worlds. Achieves a **14.7% CAGR** (matching the 2x Benchmark) but with only **-37% Max Drawdown** (vs the -85% of static 2x leverage). Uses Ratchet Logic for superior recovery.

Benchmark DJP (1x)

Buy-and-hold the iPath Bloomberg Commodity Index. Included as a reference for the commodity component used in "Safeties" strategies.

5. Risk Controls

Trend Filter (200-day SMA)

If the S&P 500 price closes below its 200-day Simple Moving Average, all strategies automatically revert to the Safe Tier (T0). This overrides both the drawdown tiers and the ratchet lock-in. The 200-day SMA is the most widely used long-term trend indicator — being below it signals a structural bear market where leverage is dangerous.

Look-Ahead Bias Prevention

All signals (drawdown level, SMA comparison) use the previous day's close via shift(1). This ensures that every trade decision is made using only information that was available at the time — no "future peeking."

6. Performance Indicators (KPIs) — What Each Column Means

IndicatorFormulaWhat It Tells You

7. How to Use This Dashboard

  1. Select a date range using the date pickers at the top. The charts and metrics will instantly recalculate for that window.
  2. Click any column header in the leaderboard table to sort by that metric (e.g., click "CAGR" to rank strategies by annualized return).
  3. Top chart (Linear %): Shows cumulative return as a simple percentage. Best for comparing final outcomes.
  4. Bottom chart (Log Scale): Shows growth on a logarithmic scale. Best for comparing relative performance across time — a straight line means consistent compounding.
  5. Hover over lines to see exact values at any point in time.
  6. Use the Reset button to return to the full historical view.

8. Advanced Analytics Guide

The dashboard now includes a professional-grade risk analytics suite below the main performance table:

  • Historical Drawdown (Underwater): This waterfall chart shows how far each strategy fell from its previous peak. It visualizes the "psychological pain" of staying invested. 0% means the strategy was at an All-Time High.
  • Rolling 1-Year Volatility: Instead of a single number, this plot shows how risk levels changed over time. Leveraged strategies show massive spikes in volatility during market turbulence, while the S&P 500 stays relatively flat.
  • Calendar Year Returns: A year-by-year comparison. This is the ultimate "report card" for identifying which strategies dominated specific market regimes (e.g., the 2008 crash vs. the 2020 recovery).

During high-inflation periods (like 2021–2022), you will notice the Real Performance chart dipping or flattening even if nominal prices are steady. This chart is the ultimate judge of whether a strategy is truly building wealth or just keeping pace with rising prices.

9. Custom Strategy Laboratory Guide

The Laboratory allows you to break away from precomputed models and test your own parameters in real-time. Unlike the main variants which are calculated on the server, the Lab runs a **high-performance simulation engine directly in your browser** using raw ticker data.

  • Dynamic Bounds: Set the specific drawdown levels that trigger leverage tiers. Narrower bounds (e.g., 2% / 5%) create a hyper-responsive strategy.
  • Interactive Allocation Table: Click "Configure Allocations" to define exactly how much of each asset (VOO, SSO, SPYU, DJP, BILL) is held at each tier. The system automatically normalizes your weights to 100%.
  • Logic Toggles: Test the impact of path-dependency (Ratchet) or trend-following (SMA200) on your specific configuration.
  • Persistence: Your laboratory "recipe" is saved to your browser's localStorage, so your data persists even after closing the tab.
